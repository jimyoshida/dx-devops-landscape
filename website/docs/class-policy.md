# Classification Policy

This document maps this technical skill classification (12 sections) to the 6 talent roles defined in the IPA "Digital Skills Standard for Promotion (DSS-P) v2.0" to indicate the level of importance for each role.

The classification is structured such that sections 1 to 6 basically cover the skill items defined in the DSS-P, while sections 7 to 12 represent an extended scope to provide a more comprehensive technical foundation.

## The 6 Roles

The 6 talent roles defined in the IPA "Digital Skills Standard for Promotion (DSS-P) 2.0" are these.

- **BA (Business Architect):** Defines the goals to be achieved through business and operational transformation, designs optimal business models and processes from a management perspective, translates strategy into action while generating growth cycles, and coordinates stakeholders to lead the full process toward outcomes.
- **Des (Designer):** Integrates business, customer/user, and communication perspectives to formulate product and service policies and development processes, handles communication design including brand messages and touchpoints, and shapes the design of products and services in alignment with these directions.
- **DS (Data Scientist):** Designs, implements, and operates data analysis and AI systems to drive business transformation and create new business value through data utilization in the promotion of DX.
- **DM (Data Management):** Designs, implements, and operates mechanisms to ensure data safety and reliability and enable continuous data circulation, involves all staff across the organization in data utilization, and promotes value creation through data.
- **SE (Software Engineer):** Responsible for designing, implementing, and operating systems and software to provide products and services using digital technology in the promotion of DX. Their core strength lies in turning plans into concrete outcomes by leveraging specific technical capabilities across the full software lifecycle.
- **Sec (Cybersecurity):** Takes measures to suppress the impact of cybersecurity risks in the digital environment that supports business processes.

Note that *DA (DevOps Architect)* is included in *SE (Software Engineer)*: Bridges development and operations by designing CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure automation, and observability platforms. As an Agile practitioner, they champion continuous delivery, iterative improvement, and cross-functional collaboration — embedding DevSecOps practices into each sprint cycle to accelerate safe software delivery.

### Legend

- **4 (Must Have)**: Core skills and knowledge areas essential for the role.
- **3 (Nice to Have)**: Areas desirable for execution or necessary for collaboration.
- **2 (Partially Relevant / Specialized)**: Relevant in specific contexts/projects or requires basic understanding.
- **1**: Low direct relevance or outside the area of expertise.

## Advice for Usage

So the matrix says SE is the widest-ranging, "busiest" role; Designer is the most specialized.

### 1. Identifying Common Skills

Section **01** can be positioned as "Common Literacy" that should be broadly educated regardless of the role to foster an organizational culture for promoting DX.

### 2. Deepening Expertise

- **Business Architect (BA)**: Focuses heavily on **Section 01** and **12** (Economics, Psychology) for business modeling. They should also be evaluated on their "Utilization/Planning level" understanding of Security, Data, and AI (Categories 04, 05, and 06).
- **Designer (Des)**: Focus evaluation on **Categories 02, 11, and 12**, representing their core knowledge of UI (Web/Mobile/Desktop) and human psychology.
- **Data Scientist (DS)**: Focus evaluation on **Categories 05, 06, and 07**, which represent their core tools and scientific knowledge.
- **Data Management (DM)**: Focus evaluation on **Categories 03, 04, and 05** as the foundation of data engineering, governance, and security. Section 01 (project management) and Section 12 (organizational/ethical dimensions) complement the core.
- **Software Engineer (SE)**: Focus evaluation on **Categories 02, 03, 04, 05, 07, 08, 09, and 10**. (Note: Section 11 is highly specialized, so it is rated a 2 for general SEs). For those in a DevOps Architect capacity, emphasize **Section 03** (CI/CD, cloud-native infrastructure, observability) and **Section 01** (Agile delivery), with Section 04 (DevSecOps) and Section 09 (scripting/automation) as complements.
- **Cybersecurity (Sec)**: Focus evaluation on **Categories 02, 03, 04, 07, and 08**, as modern security strongly overlaps with web, cloud, and infrastructure.

## Supplementary Note: About IPA

The **IPA (Information-technology Promotion Agency, Japan)** is a policy implementation agency under the jurisdiction of Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI).
It plays a central role in Japan's national IT strategy, including **Human Resource Development** (administering national IT examinations), **Information Security Measures**, and establishing **Guidelines for Digital Transformation (DX)**.

The **"DSS-P (Digital Skills Standard for Promotion)"** referenced in this document is a public standard defined by the IPA to accelerate DX in Japanese companies. It is widely adopted by many Japanese enterprises as a benchmark for talent development and hiring.
